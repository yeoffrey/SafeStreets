We believe that every journey should end safely, and every street should be a place where people can move freely without fear. Join us in our campaign for safer roads, one step at a time. 


  


  

    

      
Halton is expected to gain 520,000 more residents by 2051

      

        These residents will need safe, accessible forms of transportation.
        Widening roads is not an option; it only provides short-term relief and
        can actually increase travel times. What we need to do is give people
        more options for getting around for work, education, and pleasure.
      

    

    

  


  

    

      

        Unhealthy and expensive, Auto-dependency has impacted our daily lives
      

      

        15% of Canadians get their recommended daily physical activity, whereas
        people who live in walkable neighbourhoods get about one hour more per
        week, and are 23% less likely to experience stressful days.
      

      

        About 50% of trips in Halton Region are at most a 20-minute bike ride
        away but are taken by car.
      

    

    

  


  

    

      
We can do better

      

        There are tried-and-true ways to design our streets and roads; A way to
        help drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians stay safe as they travel from A
        to B; A way to promote an active lifestyle. We just need to demand
        better from our region and local councils.
      

      
That is why we, Safe Streets Halton, are here.

    

    

  


  

    

      
In 2020, there were over 6000 car crashes in Halton

      

        Of these crashes, 8 were fatal and over 650 resulted in serious injury –
        this can no longer be accepted.
      

      

        In a study conducted by the region, 50% of respondents said they would
        like to walk/bike to work or to visit friends and family. Our streets,
        however, don’t make it safe to do so.
